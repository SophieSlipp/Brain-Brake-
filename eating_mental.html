Eating

The recommended caloric intake each day is 2000, although this often varies with age, amount of activity and whether you want to lose gain or maintain weight. Each day you should also be eating 5 servings of vegetables, 4 serving of fruits, 6 servings of grains, and 3 servings of dairy.

Given that your brain is always “on” and functioning there needs to be “fuel” and a source of energy for this, food is this source of fuel. Your brain functions best when given the right fuel. When you eat foods that contain plenty of minerals and antioxidants your brain functions better, it can help protect your body from waste, and it can improve mood. Things like grapes can have large amounts of things like minerals and antioxidants and eating them can help prevent the risk of heart disease and cancer along with providing a large number of vitamins, other similar things are blueberries red berries nut and especially dark green vegetables. Eating heavily processed foods and foods that contain refined sugar can harm the brain, it can affect your body's ability to regulate insulin and worsen symptoms of things like depression and other mood disorders.
